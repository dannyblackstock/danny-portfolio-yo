website redesign

Client
Academic project
Year
2013
Role
Wireframing, prototyping, heuristic evaluation, usability testing, web design and development
Tools
HTML, CSS, Git, Javascript, Zurb Foundation

I worked in a team of five to study The Weather Network’s website, and develop prototypes for a redesign based on our findings for a human-computer interaction course at Simon Fraser University.

The homepage for the Weather Network website my team and I chose to redesign.

We first performed a heuristic analysis on the website by observing users interacting with it, and then asking the users to fill out a questionnaire on their experience. Through our observations, we found that the site’s organizational design, navigation experience, and aesthetics could be improved, so we we aimed to address those issues in our designs.

Restructuring the website's information heirarchy.
Early interface sketches and paper prototypes.

I worked with my team to create conceptual designs, low-fidelity prototypes and wireframes. We came up with two final website designs, and I was responsible for implementing one of them into a prototype to be used for user-testing certain tasks.

I created Prototype A using HTML, CSS, Javascript, and the Foundation framework to help speed up the development process. After final usability testing, we found that our prototypes had greatly decreased the time needed for users to complete common tasks, such as adding a city to their favourite locations, or finding a long term forecast for their current city.
